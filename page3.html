Early Years

Vincent van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853, in Groot-Zundert, a small village in the Netherlands. He was the eldest son of a pastor and grew up in a religious and cultured family.

Career Beginnings

Before becoming a painter, Van Gogh worked as an art dealer and briefly tried to become a preacher like his father. However, he did not find success in those fields and only began painting seriously at age 27.

Artistic Life

Van Gogh’s early paintings were dark and somber. Later, after moving to France, his style changed dramatically as he embraced bright colors and dynamic brush strokes. He often painted scenes of nature, people, and everyday life.

Mental Health

Van Gogh struggled with mental health problems, including episodes of psychosis and depression. He famously cut off part of his own ear during a difficult period. Despite these challenges, he continued to paint prolifically.

Death and Legacy

Van Gogh died on July 29, 1890, from a gunshot wound, believed to be self-inflicted. After his death, his work gained huge popularity and is now celebrated worldwide as some of the greatest art ever created.
